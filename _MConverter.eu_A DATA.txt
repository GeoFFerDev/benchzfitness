**Chapter 1**

**\[THE PROBLEM AND ITS BACKGROUND\]{.smallcaps}**

**Introduction**

In the modern fitness industry, the efficient management of membership
tracking, attendance validation, and payment processing is critical for
operational success. However, many facilities still rely on traditional
manual workflows. These outdated methods are highly prone to data
errors, slow transactions, and limited accessibility, which create
severe administrative bottlenecks during peak hours. Consequently, these
manual inefficiencies lead to a poor overall user experience, directly
resulting in reduced member satisfaction and lower retention rates.

To combat these challenges, the fitness sector is increasingly adopting
digital solutions such as automated membership systems, cashless payment
gateways, and basic analytics dashboards. Despite these initial
technological advancements, critical gaps remain. Current gym systems
frequently suffer from fragmented platforms, weak data security
protocols, a lack of real-time operational analytics, and limited
integration of robust identification methods. Because existing systems
often treat these functions as isolated modules rather than a unified
ecosystem, facilities are left vulnerable to security risks,
unauthorized access, and incomplete business data.

This study provides a novel contribution to the field by developing a
cohesive, data-driven, cross-platform web application that centralizes
these operational pillars. What makes this system uniquely innovative is
its \"Double Binding\" approach, which seamlessly integrates a hybrid
physical RFID and digital QR-based identification system into a unified
profile alongside automated cashless payment processing. Unlike existing
fragmented applications, this secure, all-in-one ecosystem ensures
redundant facility access while equipping administrators with
predictive, real-time analytics for member retention and revenue
tracking.

The core features of this system are deeply embedded with global
sustainability objectives. By streamlining facility access and improving
the user experience, the system actively encourages regular physical
participation, directly advancing Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 3:
Good Health and Well-being. Furthermore, deploying a secure,
cross-platform infrastructure with integrated digital finance and hybrid
identification directly supports the technological innovation targets of
SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure.

Ultimately, this study aims to design, develop, and evaluate a
comprehensive, data-driven cross-platform web application that
seamlessly integrates gym membership validation, cashless payment
processing, and advanced analytics management.

**Review of Related Literature**

This section presents the literature reviewed by the researchers to gain
better insights into the concepts covered in this study.

**Digital Transformation in Fitness Facility Operations**

The digital transformation of the fitness industry has fundamentally
shifted how facilities handle their daily operations, moving away from
error-prone manual logbooks toward automated, cloud-based
infrastructures. Recent literature firmly establishes that digitizing
gym operations significantly improves efficiency and user satisfaction.
Studies by Hussain et al. (2025) and Alboneh & Snae (2025) demonstrated
that web-based platforms for registration and management directly
enhance administrative productivity and member engagement. By automating
data entry, these systems reduce the administrative burden on gym staff,
allowing them to focus on customer service rather than tedious
paperwork.

**Identification Systems and Access Control**

A critical limitation in these existing systems is their reliance on a
single method of identification, utilizing either a digital log or a
manual check, which creates operational loopholes. This disconnection
between physical tracking and web databases often results in inaccurate
attendance records or allows for \"buddy punching,\" where members share
credentials to bypass entry fees.

QR code generation for member access has been demonstrated as a fast and
cost-effective alternative to physical credentials in gym management
contexts, enabling seamless member verification without specialized
hardware beyond a standard camera scanner (Villanueva et al., 2025).
However, systems that rely exclusively on digital QR credentials expose
a critical single point of failure, as members without functional
smartphones or with depleted device batteries are entirely denied
facility access (Villanueva et al., 2025). The Bench-Z Fitness Gym
application resolves this gap by employing a \"Double Binding\" approach
that simultaneously assigns a physical RFID keychain and a digital QR
token to every verified member profile, ensuring that a redundant
fallback credential is always available regardless of device
availability.

RFID-based attendance automation has been shown to significantly reduce
human error and shorten entry wait times in web-based gym management
systems compared to manual logbook methods (Suryawanshi et al., 2024).
However, deploying RFID as a standalone access credential introduces a
security vulnerability, as single-token systems remain susceptible to
card cloning and relay-attack exploits in the absence of a secondary
server-side verification layer (Suryawanshi et al., 2024). The Bench-Z
Fitness Gym application resolves this limitation through a
dual-credential architecture where each scanned RFID Unique Identifier
(UID) is matched against a server-verified member profile in real time,
ensuring that a cloned or stolen card cannot independently grant
unauthorized facility access.

**Modernization of Financial Transactions and Security**

The financial modernization of fitness facilities has heavily leaned
toward digital transactions, especially with the widespread adoption of
e-wallets. Bulaclac et al. (2025) proved that integrating QR-enabled
cashless payments reduced transaction times by 80% and error rates to
just 2%. This is particularly relevant when standardizing payments
through APIs like PayMongo, which ensure interoperability and seamless
transaction logging. Furthermore, Adegbite (2025) noted the importance
of standard data protection in digital finance. Without robust
encryption like AES-256, digital payment systems remain vulnerable to
interception. Despite these advancements, many small-scale gym
applications documented in current research function without unified
validation mechanisms. These systems often treat software management and
payment processing separately, creating a fragmented user experience
where financial data is not smoothly tied to physical facility access.

Mobile wallet adoption among Filipino consumers, particularly Generation
X, is primarily driven by perceived ease of use, embedded security
features, and peer social influence, establishing GCash-based digital
payment as a mainstream transaction method in the Philippine market
(Cacas et al., 2022). However, existing adoption literature evaluates
cashless behavior primarily in general retail contexts, leaving a
significant gap in how digital payment systems integrate specifically
into membership-based service platforms where payment must directly
trigger account access and subscription renewal (Cacas et al., 2022).
The Bench-Z Fitness Gym application bridges this gap by embedding the
PayMongo API natively into the membership lifecycle, ensuring that QR
Ph-standard digital payments function as a fully integrated trigger for
member account status updates rather than a standalone external checkout
process.

The sustained adoption of digital payment tools among micro-business
owners in the Philippines is primarily contingent on perceived security,
with merchants who trust their payment provider\'s fraud detection and
data protection capabilities being significantly more committed to
long-term cashless operations (Pleno, 2024). However, most
micro-businesses lack the internal technical capacity to independently
implement compliant payment security protocols, creating a structural
dependence on the security posture of their chosen third-party payment
aggregator (Pleno, 2024). The Bench-Z Fitness Gym application addresses
this structural reality by leveraging PayMongo\'s PCI DSS Level
1-certified and SOC 2 Type 2-audited infrastructure, delegating all
transaction-layer cryptographic security to a nationally recognized
compliant gateway without requiring the gym to independently maintain a
dedicated secure payment server.

**Data Analytics for Proactive Gym Management**

Data utilization has evolved into a cornerstone of modern business
management, enabling a shift from reactive to proactive decision-making.
Acepcion et al. (2025) demonstrated that predictive analytics are
essential for high-level decision-making, noting that technical experts
favor these systems for their high degree of accuracy. While such
systems allow administrators to identify profitable membership tiers
through attendance and payment logs, a significant gap remains regarding
operational granularity. Previous research primarily emphasizes general
data accuracy, leaving a practical need for integrated systems that
specifically track real-time retention metrics and staff allocation.
Most existing platforms fail to visualize peak-hour congestion or
automatically flag \"at-risk\" members with declining attendance,
features that are critical for sustaining gym profitability and central
to the current study\'s analytics framework.

**Cross-Platform Accessibility in Management Systems**

Cross-platform accessibility has emerged as a fundamental requirement
for modern management systems, primarily through the use of unified
codebases. Jošt & Taneski (2025) confirmed that cross-platform
development provides a cost-effective strategy for delivering functional
features across diverse devices while maintaining high performance. This
architecture ensures a consistent user experience, whether the portal is
accessed via a mobile browser or a desktop terminal. However, current
literature frequently treats these accessibility features as isolated
modules rather than a fully integrated ecosystem. A significant
technological gap remains in the simultaneous deployment of physical and
digital credentials to ensure identification reliability. Unlike
existing systems that separate software management from payment
processing, the current industry lacks a cohesive framework that binds a
physical RFID keychain and a digital QR code to a unified member
profile. This redundancy is critical; it ensures that if a physical tag
is misplaced, the digital alternative allows for uninterrupted facility
access and operational continuity.

**Software Quality Evaluation (ISO 25010)**

The ISO/IEC 25010:2023 standard defines eight core product quality
characteristics Functional Suitability, Performance Efficiency,
Compatibility, Interaction Capability, Reliability, Security,
Maintainability, and Flexibility that collectively measure the degree to
which a software system meets its stakeholders\' stated and implied
needs, with research on the standard reaching its highest recorded
publication volume in 2023 and its most critical applications found in
business management tools across public administration, finance, and
healthcare (Rojas et al., 2025). Security and Interaction Capability
were identified as the two characteristics most consistently deficient
in existing business-facing web applications, as these systems handle
sensitive personal and financial data demanding robust access control
and intuitive usability simultaneously (Rojas et al., 2025). The Bench-Z
Fitness Gym application is evaluated against ISO/IEC 25010:2023 with
primary emphasis on Security validated through its AES-encrypted data
layer and PayMongo-compliant payment processing and Interaction
Capability, ensuring both gym administrators and members can navigate
the system efficiently without requiring prior technical expertise.

Among the ISO/IEC 25010:2023 characteristics, Functional Suitability,
Reliability, Interaction Capability, and Security have been identified
as the most practically critical dimensions for business-facing web
applications, with Security enhancements such as two-factor
authentication and data encryption consistently ranking as the most
recommended improvements by end users in post-evaluation assessments
(Nugraha & Rachman, 2024). However, applying ISO/IEC 25010:2023
retrospectively to an already-deployed application constrains the
utility of the evaluation, as identified security gaps can only be
flagged as post-deployment recommendations rather than resolved during
the active development cycle (Nugraha & Rachman, 2024). The Bench-Z
Fitness Gym application addresses this limitation by treating ISO/IEC
25010:2023 quality criteria as prospective design benchmarks from the
earliest Agile sprint, embedding Security and Interaction Capability
requirements directly into the feature backlog to ensure they are
resolved during development rather than after deployment.

**Agile Scrum in Web Development**

Agile Scrum has been established as the preferred development
methodology for responsive, multi-module web systems because its
iterative sprint structure enables continuous validation of inter-module
integration and stakeholder requirements across short development
cycles, significantly reducing the compounding risk of late-stage
defects that are characteristic of sequential Waterfall-based approaches
(Jošt & Taneski, 2025). Agile methodologies record a project failure
rate of 10% compared to 30% for Waterfall, and successful completion
rates of 40% versus only 15% for Waterfall in comparable software
environments, a disparity attributed to Agile\'s capacity to identify
and resolve integration issues within sprints rather than after months
of irreversible sequential development (Almajali et al., 2023). The
Bench-Z Fitness Gym application is built using the Agile Scrum framework
precisely because its three integrated subsystems the hybrid RFID/QR
access module, the PayMongo cashless payment module, and the membership
analytics dashboard each require continuous incremental testing against
actual gym staff workflows, a degree of adaptive validation that a rigid
Waterfall sequence cannot accommodate without incurring costly
late-stage rework.

Agile-based iterative development cycles are uniquely suited to
cross-platform management systems because short sprint-feedback loops
allow developers to continuously validate feature parity and interface
consistency across different device classes before the system is
committed to a production release (Jošt & Taneski, 2025). However,
existing cross-platform Agile research focuses primarily on rendering
and performance dimensions without examining how Scrum\'s sprint
structure specifically accommodates hardware-dependent features such as
RFID readers and QR code scanners within a web-based environment (Jošt &
Taneski, 2025). The Bench-Z Fitness Gym application addresses this gap
by allocating dedicated Agile sprints to the hardware-interfacing
modules specifically the RFID reader communication layer and the
camera-based QR scanner integration ensuring that physical device
compatibility is treated as a first-class sprint deliverable rather than
a last-mile implementation concern.

**Synthesis**

This comprehensive review justifies the focus of the present study. The
literature confirms that while automation, cashless payments, and
analytics are individually beneficial, there is a clear necessity for a
unified solution that connects these distinct functions. Existing
systems successfully handle payments or registrations independently but
fail to provide a unified ecosystem that secures facility access against
credential sharing while simultaneously generating specialized retention
and allocation data. The proposed system aims to fill this gap by
creating a data-driven ecosystem that centralizes membership validation
and operational management, addressing the practical needs of modern
fitness centers. By introducing a cohesive hybrid multi-modal
identification system, this project resolves the operational loopholes
left by previous single-method applications.

**Conceptual Framework**

The conceptual framework of this study is structured using the Agile
Scrum Methodology, illustrating the logical progression from
foundational gym requirements to the realized software solution. The
Scrum framework supports iterative development, enabling the system to
evolve through continuous stakeholder feedback, modular feature
delivery, and regular quality evaluation.

The Agile Scrum Model consists of the Requirements Analysis, Design,
Development, Testing, and Deployment Phases. Monitoring and maintenance
will occur after the system is deployed.

The development process begins with Requirements Analysis. During this
phase, the proponents analyzed the operational bottlenecks of
traditional fitness facilities, specifically focusing on the
inefficiencies of manual logbooks and the security loopholes of
single-method identification, such as \"buddy punching.\" From this
analysis, functional requirements were established, including a member
self-service portal, a cashless payment module utilizing the PayMongo
API (Tier 1), and a membership analytics dashboard. Crucially, the
non-functional requirements were also defined, emphasizing data
protection through AES-256 encryption, Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
for administrative access, and cross-platform accessibility. Through
continuous Product Backlog Refinement, these requirements were detailed
and prioritized to ensure strict alignment with the project\'s
objectives.

Following this, the project enters the Design and Development (Sprints)
phases. The system architecture was constructed using the Laravel
Framework (PHP) for robust backend routing and a MySQL database for
centralized data storage. Development will be executed in iterative
sprints, with a heavy focus on hardware-software interfacing. Rather
than relying on motorized gates or electronic door strikes, the system
will interface RFID readers and camera-enabled QR scanners directly with
the web application to serve as an automated attendance and verification
kiosk. This will ensure the \"double binding\" authentication logic
successfully links a physical RFID Unique Identifier (UID) and a digital
QR token to a single, secure member profile.

Once the functional modules are built, the system will undergo rigorous
testing. IT experts will be tapped to evaluate the technical
architecture against the ISO 25010:2023 Software Quality Standards,
specifically assessing Functional Suitability, Performance Efficiency,
Compatibility, Interaction Capability, Reliability, Security,
Maintainability, and Portability. Simultaneously, end-users, comprising
Gym Owners and Members, will be asked to evaluate the practical
application, focusing on Functional Suitability, Interaction Capability,
Reliability, and Security. Feedback that will be obtained during these
evaluations will be incorporated into sprint retrospectives to resolve
any software defects or usability issues.

Upon successful validation, the system will advance to Deployment. The
culmination of this methodological cycle is the Gym web-based
application: a secure, data-driven, cross-platform web application that
effectively centralizes membership validation, automates QR Ph standard
payments, and equips gym administrators with predictive analytics for
retention and revenue tracking.

Following the system\'s deployment, the project transitions into the
Monitoring and Maintenance phase. During this stage, the deployed web
application undergoes continuous observation to ensure server stability,
database integrity, and the smooth execution of the PayMongo cashless
transactions and hybrid identification hardware. Furthermore, feedback
from gym members and administrators is actively gathered to identify any
post-deployment usability issues or emerging operational bottlenecks.
Because the system follows the Agile framework, any required bug fixes,
security patches, or feature enhancements identified during maintenance
are continuously fed back into the Product Backlog to be addressed in
future development sprints.

**Scope and Delimitations**

The primary focus of this study is to develop a specialized management
system for gym operations that emphasizes data integrity, security, and
cross-platform accessibility. The scope centers on the development of a
hybrid multimodal identification system that implements a double-binding
check-in process. In this setup, members will be issued both a unique
QR-based digital ID and a physical RFID-enabled keychain, both of which
are bound to a single member profile to ensure redundant and flexible
facility access. This will be supported by hardware-software
interfacing, with the system integrating RFID readers and camera-based
QR code scanners that communicate directly with the Laravel-based web
application for real-time verification.

Functionally, the system will include a comprehensive membership
management module capable of digital registration and real-time status
tracking across tiers, including full membership, monthly passes, and
walk-in or day passes. This module will automatically manage active,
expired, and suspended statuses based on validation logic. To empower
end-users, the system will include a member self-service portal that
allows clients to access session history, view discounts, and manage
profile information. Financial operations will be streamlined through a
cashless payment module utilizing the PayMongo API (Tier 1). This
integration will facilitate automated transactions via QR Ph standards
and maintain a detailed transaction history specifically designed for
administrative auditing. Furthermore, the system will aid
decision-making through an advanced membership analytics dashboard,
which provides visual summaries of occupancy trends, retention metrics
to identify at-risk members, and insights into the revenue contribution
of different membership plans. All these features will be protected by a
strict security framework that uses AES-256 encryption for sensitive
data and multi-factor authentication (MFA) for administrative functions.

Despite these capabilities, the study will observe specific technical
boundaries to maintain its focus on web and systems technologies. The
research excludes the integration of heavy physical hardware, such as
automated turnstiles, electronic door strikes, or motorized gates, and
focuses strictly on software-side validation of RFID and QR credentials.
Furthermore, the system does not incorporate biometric authentication
technologies, such as fingerprint, iris, or facial recognition. In terms
of financial integration, the study is limited to e-wallet and QR Ph
verification via PayMongo; it will not directly interface with bank
accounts or credit card processors. Additionally, as a cloud-hosted web
application, it requires a stable internet connection and does not
support offline data entry or local database synchronization. Finally,
the study does not cover ancillary management systems, such as employee
payroll processing, inventory management for gym merchandise, or
point-of-sale (POS) systems for physical products.

**Significance of the Study**

The development of this data-driven, cross-platform web application has
significant value for stakeholders in the fitness and technology sectors
by modernizing gym membership management with secure, efficient
technologies. Specifically, the output and methodology of this study
will benefit the following groups:

**Gym Owners and Administrators.** Through the output of this study, a
centralized, automated web platform, this group will gain an efficient
tool for managing memberships, attendance, and payments of gym members.
In practice, by using a hybrid RFID and QR-based identification system,
they will significantly reduce manual administrative errors and prevent
unauthorized facility access. Furthermore, they will gain practical
decision-making tools through the integrated analytics dashboard, which
provides data-driven insights into gym occupancy and revenue trends to
support strategic business growth.

**Gym Members.** This group will benefit practically by experiencing a

flexible access options, members can utilize either physical RFID
keychains or digital QR IDs to enter the facility. Additionally, they
will gain the practical advantage of using the cashless payment module,
which reduces queues and eliminates the risks of manual cash handling.
Ultimately, members will be assured of accurate attendance tracking and
the strict protection of their personal and financial data through the
system\'s robust security protocols.

**Information Technology Professionals and Developers.** This group will

study as a technical reference. The output demonstrates best practices
in developing cross-platform applications that successfully integrate

executing the Agile Scrum Methodology and evaluating software
architecture against rigorous ISO 25010:2023 standards, specifically
regarding system security and maintainability.

**Future Researchers.** This group will benefit from the foundational
output and methodological framework established by this research. By
analyzing the system\'s design and identifying delimitations, future
researchers can practically extend this work. They are provided with a
proven baseline that they can enhance by incorporating more advanced
technologies, such as biometric authentication, deeper predictive
analytics, or offline database functionality, to further advance gym
management systems.

**Definition of Terms**

The following terms are conceptually and operationally defined to
provide the meaning of the terms used in this study.

**Agile Scrum Methodology.** An iterative and incremental software
development framework is employed to manage complex product development
processes, promoting adaptability, collaboration, and continuous
improvement (Hussain et al., 2025). In this study, the term refers to
the structured development phases, known as sprints, that guided the
creation of the gym management system, emphasizing continuous
stakeholder feedback, modular feature delivery, and regular quality
evaluation.

**AES-256 Encryption.** A robust encryption method employing a 256-bit
cryptographic key to securely encode and decode digital data, thereby
safeguarding sensitive information against cyber threats (Adegbite,
2025). In this study, the term refers to the specific security protocol
implemented within the system to protect the personal, financial, and
authentication data of gym members from unauthorized access or potential
breaches.

**Cashless Payment Module.** A digital financial system, often
QR-enabled, that facilitates electronic monetary transactions and
tracking without the need for physical currency, enhancing convenience
and transaction security (Bulaclac et al., 2025). In this study, the
term refers to the system component that integrates the PayMongo API
(Tier 1) to enable secure QR PH-enabled e-wallet payments for gym
services, while simultaneously recording transactions for administrative
auditing and accountability.

**Cross-Platform Web Application.** A software application designed to
function across multiple computing platforms or operating systems using
a single, unified codebase, while maintaining near-native performance
(Jošt & Taneski, 2025). In this study, the term refers to the
browser-based gym management system developed to operate consistently
across both desktop and mobile web environments, ensuring seamless
access and usability for staff and members.

**Data-Driven Management System.** An administrative framework that
leverages predictive analytics and continuous data analysis to enhance
operational efficiency and inform business decision-making (Acepcion et
al., 2025). In this study, the term refers to the web-based system
developed for the gym, which processes operational and membership data
to generate real-time predictive metrics and support informed
administrative decision-making.

**Double Binding (Hybrid Multi-Modal Identification).** A redundant
authentication mechanism that requires two distinct and parallel forms
of identification linked to a single entity, enhancing security and
access control (El-Rahman & Alluhaidan, 2024). In this study, the term
refers to the core authentication logic that simultaneously binds a
physical RFID Unique Identifier (UID) and a digitally generated QR token
to a single member profile, ensuring secure and continuous facility
access.

**Functional Modules.** Distinct, independently executable software
components that collectively form a larger and more comprehensive system
architecture, enabling modularity, scalability, and maintainability
within software systems (Kolhe & Sharma, 2021). In this study, the term
refers to the core system components developed during the Agile sprints,
specifically including the Membership Management module, Hybrid Identity
Binding module, Cashless Payment Integration module, Analytics
Dashboard, and the Security Framework.

**Gym Membership Management System.** A specialized web-based software
system designed to support fitness facilities in the remote management
of member information, digital payment transactions, and routine
operational processes, thereby enhancing administrative efficiency and
service delivery (Alboneh & Snae, 2025). In this study, the term refers
to the integrated, cross-platform web application developed as the
primary system output, which automates key functions such as member
registration, data validation, attendance tracking, and payment
processing within the fitness facility.

**Hardware-Software Interfacing.** The point of communication and
automated data exchange between physical hardware devices and programmed
software applications (Kolhe & Sharma, 2021). In this study, the term
refers to the integration process that enables the gym\'s RFID readers
and camera-based QR scanners to communicate in real time with the
Laravel-based web application for membership verification.

**Hybrid Multi-Modal Identification System.** A security access system
that utilizes multiple types of credentials (e.g., physical tokens and
digital codes) to ensure strict user verification (El-Rahman &
Alluhaidan, 2024). In this study, the term refers to the primary system
output that combines physical RFID-based access and digital QR-based
verification, preventing unauthorized entry and eliminating credential
sharing (buddy punching).

**ISO 25010:2023 Software Quality Standards.** An internationally
recognized framework that provides comprehensive quality models for
software product evaluation, specifically assessing reliability,
efficiency, and security (International Organization for
Standardization, 2023). In this study, the term refers to the specific
evaluation model employed by IT experts to assess the application across
functional suitability, performance efficiency, security, and other key
technical characteristics.

**Laravel Framework.** A free, open-source PHP web application framework
designed for developing web applications following the
model-view-controller (MVC) architectural pattern (Kolhe & Sharma,
2021). In this study, the term refers to the backend software
architecture used to manage routing, authentication, database
interactions, and secure API integrations for the gym management system.

**Membership Analytics Dashboard.** A visual user interface that
consolidates and provides real-time access to financial information and
member data for management purposes (Alboneh & Snae, 2025). In this
study, the term refers to the system component that provides
administrators with graphical summaries of occupancy trends, revenue
performance, and member retention metrics.

**QR Code (Quick Response Code).** A machine-readable, two-dimensional
matrix barcode used to accelerate tracking and digital payment processes
(Bulaclac et al., 2025). In this study, the term refers to the unique
digital barcode generated by the application, serving as a gym member\'s
digital identifier and payment verification tool.

**QR Ph Standard.** The national Quick Response (QR) code standard in
the Philippines, designed to unify payment solutions and ensure
interoperability among financial service providers (Bangko Sentral ng
Pilipinas, 2019). In this study, the term refers to the payment standard
integrated into the system via the PayMongo API, enabling gym members to
make payments using various interoperable e-wallet providers.

**RFID (Radio Frequency Identification).** A wireless technology that
employs electromagnetic fields to automatically identify and track tags
attached to objects or individuals (Huang et al., 2022). In this study,
the term refers to the specific wireless technology utilized for
automated recognition of gym members and the logging of their
attendance.

**RFID Unique Identifier (UID).** A distinct, immutable alphanumeric
code programmed into an RFID tag during the manufacturing process (Huang
et al., 2022). In this study, the term refers to the specific
alphanumeric string permanently assigned to a registered gym member\'s
keychain for secure identification within the system\'s database.

**Retention Metrics.** Quantitative data and predictive indicators are
used by organizations to measure customer retention and monitor activity
over a specified period (Acepcion et al., 2025). In this study, the term
refers to the system-generated indicators displayed on the gym\'s
dashboard that identify patterns of declining attendance and enable
proactive follow-up with at-risk members.

**\[METHODOLOGY\]{.smallcaps}**

This chapter discusses the research design, research locale, research

the proposed study.

**Research Design**

This study will utilize a Developmental Research Design combined with a
Quantitative Descriptive Approach to design, develop, and evaluate a
data-driven gym management system.

Developmental research is appropriate for this study because it focuses
on the systematic design, development, and evaluation of technological
solutions intended to address real-world problems. In this case, the
study aims to develop a cross-platform web application that integrates
gym membership management, cashless payment processing, and membership
analytics.

The choice of developmental research is justified by the project\'s
nature, which will involve multiple phases, including system planning,
design, implementation, testing, and refinement. Unlike purely
descriptive or experimental research, this approach will emphasize the
creation of a functional system and the assessment of its effectiveness
in improving existing manual processes. The gym\'s current operations,
which rely on logbooks, manual payment tracking, and CCTV verification,
suffer from inefficiencies that require a structured system solution.

Furthermore, the study will employ a quantitative descriptive method to
evaluate the Bench-Z Fitness Gym Cross-platform App against ISO/IEC
25010 software quality standards, including functionality, usability,
reliability, and performance efficiency. Data will be collected from gym
staff and members through structured questionnaires to determine the
system\'s level of acceptability.

By combining developmental and quantitative approaches, the study
ensures that the proposed system is not only properly developed but also
objectively evaluated based on measurable criteria, making the research
both practical and evidence-based.

**Software Development Methodology**

The development of the system will follow the Agile Scrum cycle, which
emphasizes iterative development, continuous feedback, and incremental
delivery of system components. This approach will be selected to ensure
flexibility and adaptability throughout the development process,
allowing the researchers to refine system features based on evolving
requirements and identified issues.

The process will begin with the requirements and logic mapping phase,
during which the proponents will identify the essential technical and
theoretical foundations of the system. In this stage, the concept of
\"Double Binding\" will be established, referring to the relational
mapping of RFID UIDs and QR tokens to a single member account within the
database. The technical stack will also be finalized, including the
selection of the Laravel framework and MySQL for backend development,
along with the integration of the PayMongo API to support automated,
secure financial transactions.

Following this, the development will progress through iterative cycles
known as sprints, each focusing on specific functional components of the
system. The implementation will include the Hybrid Identity Binding
feature, which will enable communication between RFID readers and
camera-enabled devices with the web application for real-time membership
verification. In addition, a cashless payment module will be developed
by integrating the PayMongo API, enabling secure QR PH transactions and
automatic payment confirmation. The system will also include a data
analytics dashboard to display retention metrics and membership plan
insights, while security measures such as AES-256 encryption and
Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) will be implemented to protect
sensitive data and restrict administrative access.

At the end of each development cycle, evaluative feedback and testing
will be conducted to assess the functionality and performance of the
implemented features. The system will be evaluated against ISO/IEC 25010
standards, focusing on functional suitability, performance efficiency,
compatibility, usability, reliability, security, maintainability, and
portability. This will ensure that the system is consistently aligned
with recognized software quality benchmarks throughout the development
process.

Finally, the system will undergo deployment, resulting in a fully
functional cross-platform web application. The completed system will
serve as an integrated solution that transforms manual gym operations
into an automated environment, highlighted by the Hybrid Multi-Modal
Identification System, which will provide members with flexible and
reliable access to the facility.

The second group comprises Gym Owners and Administrators, who are the

successfully reduces the workload associated with manual tracking and

The third group consists of the Gym Members, representing the
facility\'s active clientele. The proponents will use random sampling to
select participants for testing the user-facing features. These
respondents will assess the Member Self-Service Portal, evaluating the

establishing whether the shift to digital identification and payments
enhances the overall gym experience.

**Research Instruments**

To quantitatively evaluate the quality and performance of the Bench-Z
Fitness Gym Cross-Platform Application, this study employs a structured
survey questionnaire as its primary research instrument. The instrument
is developed in accordance with ISO/IEC 25010:2023, the Software Quality
Model, ensuring that the evaluation remains objective, systematic, and
aligned with internationally recognized software engineering standards.
It comprises three distinct sets of questionnaires, each tailored to the
expertise and roles of the identified respondent groups.

The first instrument, the Technical Assessment Questionnaire, is
intended for IT professionals and web developers with relevant
subject-matter expertise. This instrument assesses the system based on
the nine software quality characteristics defined in ISO/IEC 25010:
Functional Suitability, Performance Efficiency, Compatibility,
Interaction Capability, Reliability, Security, Maintainability,
Flexibility, and Safety. These criteria determine the extent to which
the system satisfies functional requirements, performs efficiently under
expected workloads, supports cross-platform operation, and ensures data
protection through security mechanisms such as AES-256 encryption and
multi-factor authentication. Furthermore, it evaluates the system\'s
modularity and capacity for future enhancements.

The second instrument, the User Experience Questionnaire, is designed
for gym owners and members. It focuses on the practical and
interactional aspects of the application and evaluates four key quality
characteristics relevant to end users: Functional Suitability,
Usability, Reliability, and Security. Specifically, it examines whether
the system effectively supports routine gym operations, such as
attendance monitoring and payment processing; whether it is
user-friendly for individuals with limited technical expertise; whether
it operates consistently with minimal errors; and whether users perceive
their personal and financial data as secure.

The third instrument, the WCAG-Based Accessibility Evaluation Tool, is
adapted from Olipas (2026) and used to assess the web application\'s
accessibility. This instrument is grounded in the four core principles
of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG): Perceivable,
Operable, Understandable, and Robust. It ensures the application is
inclusive, accessible, and functional for users with diverse needs,
including those who use assistive technologies.

To address Research Question 4, which focuses on the application\'s
accessibility in relation to WCAG standards, data will be gathered using
both an automated web accessibility evaluation tool and a standardized
WCAG compliance checklist to ensure a comprehensive evaluation.

All instruments utilize a 5-point Likert scale to measure respondents\'
level of agreement with each quality indicator. Responses are assigned
corresponding numerical values ranging from 1 to 5, thereby enabling
quantitative analysis and supporting the derivation of statistically
grounded conclusions.

The data-gathering process of this study follows a structured,
systematic approach to ensure the validity, reliability, and ethical
integrity of the collected data. The process is divided into several
phases, namely: preparation, orientation, administration, retrieval, and
data organization.

The first phase is the preparation and approval stage. The researchers
will prepare a formal letter of request addressed to the management of
Benchz Fitness Gym, seeking permission to conduct the study within the
premises and to involve the gym owners, administrators, and members as
respondents. A separate request will also be sent to selected IT
professionals and web developers, inviting them to participate as
technical evaluators of the system. These letters will clearly state the
study\'s purpose, the procedures involved, and the expected
participation of the respondents. Upon approval, the researchers will
coordinate with gym management to schedule data collection at a
convenient time that does not disrupt regular gym operations.

The second phase involves the orientation and system demonstration.
Before administering the research instruments, the researchers will
conduct a brief orientation session for all respondents. During this
session, the study\'s purpose will be explained, and respondents will be
informed of their rights, including the option to participate
voluntarily, confidentiality, and the right to withdraw at any time.
Following the orientation, the researchers will demonstrate the
developed Cross-Platform Web Application. This includes showcasing key
system features such as the Hybrid Multi-Modal Identification System
(RFID and QR Code), the Cashless Payment Module integrated with
PayMongo, and the Membership Analytics Dashboard. This step ensures that
respondents have sufficient understanding of the system\'s
functionalities before providing their evaluation.

The third phase is the administration of the research instruments. The
appropriate questionnaire will be distributed to each group of
respondents based on their role. The Technical Assessment Questionnaire
will be given to IT professionals and web developers, while the User
Experience Questionnaire will be administered to gym owners and members.
The researchers will be present during the evaluation to clarify any
questions and ensure that the respondents complete the questionnaire
independently, without external influence.

The fourth phase is the retrieval of the instruments. To ensure a high
retrieval rate and data completeness, the researchers will collect
completed questionnaires immediately upon respondents\' completion. Each
questionnaire will be checked for completeness to avoid missing data.
Incomplete responses, if any, will be politely verified with the
respondents before final collection.

The fifth phase is the data organization and preparation for analysis.
The collected data will be systematically encoded, tallied, and
organized into tabular form. The responses will then be subjected to
appropriate statistical treatment, such as the computation of weighted
means, to facilitate analysis and interpretation. All collected data
will be handled with strict confidentiality and will be used solely for
academic purposes.

**Ethical Considerations**

This study strictly adhered to established ethical principles governing
research involving human participants, grounded in the fundamental
principles of respect for persons, beneficence, and justice, as
articulated in the Belmont Report (National Commission for the
Protection of Human Subjects of Biomedical and Behavioral Research,
1979). These principles ensured that all respondents were treated with
dignity, protected from harm, and provided equal opportunity to
participate without coercion or discrimination.

Informed Consent\*\*.\*\* Prior to system evaluation, informed consent
was explicitly obtained from all respondents. Participants were provided
with an introductory letter detailing the study\'s purpose, the
estimated time required to complete the questionnaire, and their exact
rights as research participants.

Voluntary Participation. Participation in this study was entirely
voluntary. Respondents were explicitly informed of their right to
withdraw from the evaluation at any time without facing any penalties,
coercion, or negative consequences regarding their gym membership or
professional standing.

Confidentiality and Data Privacy. In strict compliance with the Data
Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173), both the researchers and
the developed system adhered to rigorous data protection standards. All
evaluation data gathered from IT experts and gym end-users was treated
with strict confidentiality and anonymized to prevent personal
identification.

Fair Selection of Respondents. Following the principle of justice,
respondents were selected impartially and without discrimination. IT
professionals, gym administrators, and active gym members were chosen
solely based on their direct relevance to the study\'s objectives to
ensure valid and reliable evaluations.

Researcher Integrity. The researchers guarantee that all collected
survey data was reported truthfully and accurately, free from
fabrication or alteration. The data will be stored securely, used
exclusively for academic research purposes, and safely disposed of upon
the conclusion and approval of the study.

**WCAG-Based Accessibility Evaluation Instrument**

**Instructions:** Please rate the system\'s accessibility on each item
using the 5-point scale below: 5 \-- Highly Accessible \| 4 \--
Accessible \| 3 \-- Moderately Accessible \| 2 \-- Slightly Accessible
\| 1 \-- Not Accessible

  ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ ------------------------- ------------------ ----------------------------- --------------------------- ----------------------
  **Item Statements**                                                                        **5 Highly Accessible**   **4 Accessible**   **3 Moderately Accessible**   **2 Slightly Accessible**   **1 Not Accessible**
  **A. Perceivable**                                                                                                                                                                                
  1\. The developed system provides appropriate alternative text for all non-text content.                                                                                                          
  2\. Informative images include meaningful descriptions.                                                                                                                                           
  3\. Complex visuals (charts, infographics) are accompanied by textual explanations.                                                                                                               
  4\. Video content includes captions or subtitles.                                                                                                                                                 
  5\. Audio content provides transcripts.                                                                                                                                                           
  6\. Multimedia elements have accessible alternatives.                                                                                                                                             
  7\. Content structure (headings, lists, landmarks) is properly organized.                                                                                                                         
  8\. The interface remains usable when zoomed up to 200%.                                                                                                                                          
  9\. Content adapts to different screen sizes and orientations.                                                                                                                                    
  10\. Text meets recommended color contrast standards.                                                                                                                                             
  11\. Information is not conveyed by color alone.                                                                                                                                                  
  12\. Text is readable, resizable, and clearly visible.                                                                                                                                            
  **B. Operable**                                                                                                                                                                                   
  1\. All functionalities can be accessed using a keyboard.                                                                                                                                         
  2\. No keyboard traps are encountered.                                                                                                                                                            
  3\. Navigation follows a logical tab sequence.                                                                                                                                                    
  4\. Navigation menus are consistent across pages.                                                                                                                                                 
  5\. Pages have clear and descriptive titles.                                                                                                                                                      
  6\. Users can easily determine their location within the system.                                                                                                                                  
  7\. Buttons and links are easy to identify and interact with.                                                                                                                                     
  8\. Click/touch targets are appropriately sized.                                                                                                                                                  
  9\. The system supports multiple input methods (mouse, keyboard, touch).                                                                                                                          
  10\. Users can control or extend time limits.                                                                                                                                                     
  11\. Moving or blinking content can be paused or stopped.                                                                                                                                         
  12\. The system avoids flashing elements that may trigger seizures.                                                                                                                               
  **C. Understandable**                                                                                                                                                                             
  1\. Content is written in clear and simple language.                                                                                                                                              
  2\. Instructions are easy to understand.                                                                                                                                                          
  3\. Terminologies are appropriate for the target users.                                                                                                                                           
  4\. System behavior is consistent across all pages.                                                                                                                                               
  5\. Navigation and layout remain uniform.                                                                                                                                                         
  6\. Actions produce expected and logical results.                                                                                                                                                 
  7\. Forms include clear labels and instructions.                                                                                                                                                  
  8\. Errors are clearly identified and explained.                                                                                                                                                  
  9\. The system provides helpful suggestions for correcting errors.                                                                                                                                
  **D. Robust**                                                                                                                                                                                     
  1\. The system works across different browsers and devices.                                                                                                                                       
  2\. Code structure follows web standards.                                                                                                                                                         
  3\. The system remains functional under different environments.                                                                                                                                   
  4\. The system is compatible with screen readers.                                                                                                                                                 
  5\. ARIA roles and labels are properly implemented.                                                                                                                                               
  6\. Dynamic content updates are properly communicated to assistive tools.                                                                                                                         
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ ------------------------- ------------------ ----------------------------- --------------------------- ----------------------
